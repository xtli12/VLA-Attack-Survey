Abstract

Why VLA attacks require a robotics view

With the rapid advancement of Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models, ensuring the security of embodied intelligent systems has emerged as a critical area of research. This survey provides a comprehensive analysis of VLA safety, exploring the unique vulnerabilities that arise when multimodal reasoning is directly coupled with physical execution. We introduce a unified, mechanism-driven framework that integrates the entire attack lifecycle, offering a holistic perspective on how adversarial interventions manipulate perception, planning, and control. Through a systematic analysis of VLA architectures, we establish a classification framework that distinguishes direct embodied threats from traditional digital attacks, providing standardized criteria for evaluating attack success in closed-loop environments. We identify critical limitations in existing research regarding physical evaluations, privacy metrics, and adaptive safeguards, and we outline future directions to strengthen VLA robustness.

Framework

From semantic intervention to physical consequence

01

Entry Surfaces

Vision, language, training data, memory, retrieval, tools, middleware, physical context, and human interaction.

02

Target Representations

Grounding, plans, reasoning traces, action tokens, chunks, waypoints, trajectories, controller messages, memory entries, and tool calls.

03

Consequences

Task failure, targeted behavior, unsafe motion, unauthorized skill use, privacy leakage, persistence, and recovery failure.

04

Evidence Levels

Digital input, real-sensor input, simulation rollout, hardware-in-the-loop, real-robot actuation, and human-proximate validation.

The evidence appendix separates direct VLA evidence from embodied-adjacent and analogical work. A record is direct only when the target is a VLA model, VLA-controlled robot, VLA training channel, or VLA privacy channel, and the measured outcome concerns actions, trajectories, reasoning-to-action behavior, triggered policy behavior, or training-data membership.
